FamilyBenefits

Get more from being part of a family with Velocity and Virgin Australia. With things like Family Pooling, Points Transfer and Parental Pause, whether you're a solo traveler or a traveling family, we've got you covered!

Discover Velocity's Family Pooling

Family Pooling is an exclusive Velocity benefit that allows members to pool either theirPoints orPoints and Status Creditswith family members living at the same address.When you sign up for Family Pooling, youre working together to accrue all the familys Velocity Points and or Status Credits into one family members account, helping you to reach your Points and Status goals sooner.

  • Fast track your Velocity balance

    Family Pooling is the best way to fast-track your Points and Status Credit balance

  • Pool up to 6 family members

    A family pool can consist of a max of 6family membersliving at the same address

  • Add or remove members

    Manage your Family Pooling online. You can start or stop contributing to a Family Pool at any time.

  • Reward yourself with Points

    Use your Points on Reward Seats, at the Velocity Reward store or the Velocity Wine Store.

Set up Family Pooling

You must set up Family Points Pooling before your family members earn Velocity Points and Status Credits as pooling is not backdated (i.e., any existing Velocity Points and Status Credits already in your family members accounts will not be transferred after you set up Family Pooling).

Who can be in a Family Pool

A Family Pool can consist of up to 6 family members(including the beneficiary) that are living at the sameaddress, including a maximum of 2 adults over theage of 18 and then up to 4 children under 18. Notethat if there are already two adults in a Family Pooland a child in the Family Pool turns 18, then thatmember would be classed as a third adult and wouldthen be automatically removed from the FamilyPool.

How to set up a Family Pool

    • In order to opt into a Family Pool as a contributor, first make sure you are logged in and then click on Set up Family Pool link below or in the Link & Transfer section of My Velocity.
    • Select whether or not you would like to pool Points only or pool both Points and Status Credits to the beneficiarys account.
    • Enter the details of your beneficiary, including your relationship to them.
    • Confirm the details are correct and hit submit request.
    • All done - Points & or Status Credits will begin to pool from the following day onwards.

    Only the contributors to a Family Pool can opt in or out and the must do so from their own account.

    • Make sure youre logged in and navigate to the Link & Transfer > Family Points Pooling page in My Velocity.
    • Here you will see the details of your Family Pool.
    • Simply press Stop Pooling next to the name of your current beneficiary.
    • All done - Points & or Status Credits will stop pooling into the beneficiarys account from the following day onwards.

Family Points transfers

Share the love with Points

Parental pause for parental leave

Expecting a new addition to your family?

You can pause your Platinum, Gold or Silver membership for six months and pick up where you left off with your membership level intact.

Membership Pause applies to the membership level and does not apply to Points and Status Credits.

Members who are approved for Membership Pause can continue to earn and redeem Points and Status Credits, and existing Status Credits will continue to be removed from their membership account, once Status Credits reach their 12 month validity.

All members are entitled to apply for a Membership Pause for Parental Leave once for each child, up until that child reaches the age of two years. There may be up to two member parents on Membership Pause for Parental Leave, for the same child, at any one time.

See Membership Pause for Parental LeaveTerms and Conditions.

To apply for a Membership Pause, members should call the Membership Contact Centre and may be required to provide documentation of pregnancy, recent or future child birth or adoption of a child in the form of a doctor's certificate; a birth certificate; or an adoption or foster parent registration.
